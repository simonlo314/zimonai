USB‑C is not a promise.

What to check before you trust a charger — without lab equipment.

Standards Apr 27, 2026 6 min read
← Back to Blog

USB‑C looks universal, but the connector shape tells you almost nothing about the quality of power inside a charger. Two bricks can both “support fast charge” yet behave very differently under load.

1) Look for real standards, not slogans

Prefer products that clearly state a recognized standard (for example, USB Power Delivery / PD) and list supported profiles. Marketing phrases like “super fast” without specifics are a common red flag.

2) Check wattage *and* the profiles

Wattage alone is not enough. A 65W label doesn’t guarantee stable delivery across all profiles. When possible, verify that the charger supports the profile your device expects.

3) Avoid suspiciously cheap multi‑port “everything chargers”

Multi‑port designs are harder to engineer well. If the price looks too good for the claimed power, it usually is. Power sharing logic and heat management are where corners get cut.

4) Heat is information

Warm is normal, but “too hot to touch” is a warning signal. Excess heat can indicate inefficient conversion, poor internal components, or unstable regulation under load.

5) Don’t treat cables as interchangeable

For USB‑C, cable quality matters. Use reputable, appropriately rated cables for the power you’re drawing. If a cable feels loose, shows discoloration, or behaves inconsistently, replace it.
