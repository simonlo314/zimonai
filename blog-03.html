A procurement checklist.

For teams buying chargers & cables — consistent, easy to adopt.

Procurement Apr 27, 2026 8 min read
← Back to Blog

Teams often standardize laptops and phones — then treat power accessories as “cheap and interchangeable”. This checklist helps you buy fewer, better SKUs with fewer surprises.

1) Define your device classes

List the common device types in your org (e.g., laptops, tablets, phones, peripherals). Procurement gets easier when accessories map to a known set of power needs.

2) Prefer standards and documented profiles

Look for clear support statements for charging standards (such as USB‑PD) and avoid ambiguous “fast charge” marketing. If the spec sheet is vague, treat it as a risk signal.

3) Cable policy: rating, length, and connectors

Decide what ratings you allow, acceptable lengths, and connector durability requirements. Cables are a frequent failure point and the easiest to standardize.

4) Heat and enclosure design matter

Favor chargers with credible thermal design and safety markings, not ultra-compact bricks claiming extreme power. If the charger runs very hot in normal use, treat it as non-compliant.

5) Operationalize it

Write a one-page internal rule: approved brands/models, replacement intervals, and a process for exceptions. Most teams win by being consistent, not by being perfect.
