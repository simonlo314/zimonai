Battery health myths.

What “100%” really costs — and what actually matters.

Batteries Apr 27, 2026 6 min read
← Back to Blog

Battery health advice online is often presented as strict rules. In reality, most of the impact comes from a few themes: heat, time at high charge, and how hard you push the battery day after day.

Myth: “Always charge to 100% is fine”

For many lithium batteries, spending long periods near full charge increases stress compared to stopping earlier. This doesn’t mean “never hit 100%” — it means avoid living there all day, every day.

Myth: “Fast charging destroys batteries”

Fast charging is a tool. Good devices and good chargers manage heat and current dynamically. The bigger risk is poor-quality power and sustained heat, not the concept of “fast”.

Heat is the multiplier

Heat accelerates wear. If your device is hot while charging (especially under heavy use), battery aging is more likely to speed up. If you can, charge in a cooler environment and avoid heavy workloads during charging.

Practical habits that don’t require obsession

Use reputable chargers/cables, avoid “too hot to touch” charging sessions, and don’t keep a device at full charge for long periods unnecessarily. Consistency beats perfection.
